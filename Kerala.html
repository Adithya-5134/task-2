Kerala

Kerala Backwaters – A unique network of lagoons and lakes

Quick Facts

  • Area: 38,863 Sq. Kms
  • Capital: Thiruvananthapuram
  • Language: Malayalam
  • Population: 3,34,06,061
  • Famous for: Backwaters, Ayurveda, Spices, Beaches, Hill Stations (Munnar, Wayanad), Kathakali, Elephants
  • Major Cities: Kochi, Thiruvananthapuram, Kozhikode

Geographical & Economic Importance

Kerala is known for its lush greenery, backwaters, and Western Ghats. The state has a tropical monsoon climate and is a major producer of spices, rubber, and coconut.

It has a 100% literacy rate, one of the highest in India, and is a major hub for Ayurvedic medicine.

Historical Significance

Kerala has been a center for trade since ancient times, attracting traders from Rome, Greece, China, and the Middle East.

The region played a crucial role in the Spice Trade, with ports like Muziris being prominent in global commerce.

It is also home to some of India's oldest temples, churches, and mosques, including the Padmanabhaswamy Temple and the Cheraman Juma Masjid, India's first mosque.

Cultural Significance

Kerala's cultural identity is deeply rooted in its traditions and arts.

  • Onam Festival: The state's biggest harvest festival, featuring boat races and traditional feasts.
  • Kathakali: A classical dance-drama known for its colorful costumes and expressive movements.
  • Mohiniyattam: A graceful classical dance that originated in Kerala.
  • Kerala Cuisine: Famous for Sadya, Puttu, Appam, Kerala Parotta, and Malabar Biryani.

Tourist Attractions

  • Munnar: A beautiful hill station with tea plantations and cool weather.
  • Alleppey: Known for its backwaters and houseboat cruises.
  • Kovalam Beach: A popular beach destination with golden sands.
  • Wayanad: Famous for its wildlife sanctuaries and waterfalls.
  • Athirappilly Falls: The largest waterfall in Kerala, often called the 'Niagara of India'.

Interesting Facts

  • Kerala is known as "God's Own Country" due to its natural beauty and rich heritage.
  • It has the highest life expectancy in India.
  • Kerala has a matrilineal system in some communities, where lineage is traced through the mother.
  • The state produces over 95% of India’s black pepper.
